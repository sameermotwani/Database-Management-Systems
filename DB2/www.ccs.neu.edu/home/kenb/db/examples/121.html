Sample Exercise

Design a database for a social networking site. A person may have friends who are other persons. Such a friendship relationship has a date when it started and possibly a date when it ended. A person has a name, may have an image, and may have interests. A person can belong to groups. The database must keep track of when the person belonged to each group. A group has a name and may contain other groups. A person can join and leave a group more than once, and the site must keep track of this. This is not the case for friendship relationships.

Show your design as a UML diagram. Show all cardinality constraints on both associations and attributes, and label both ends of each association. See UML Role Names for what these labels mean. Use any tool for your diagram, but submit the diagram as an image rather than some specialized file type of your tool. If you cannot get a suitable tool, then draw the diagram on paper, and either submit it to the TA or scan it and email it.

The default multiplicity for an attribute is 1..1 and does not have to be shown. Associations do not have default cardinality constraints. For an association, if it is not specified that a property (i.e., one end of an association) is mandatory, then it is optional.
