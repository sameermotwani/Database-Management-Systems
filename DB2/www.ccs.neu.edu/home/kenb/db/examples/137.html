Role Names in UML

The role names for the ends of an association have been part of UML class diagrams for a long time, but common use of this feature is relatively recent. For a long time associations were labeled with a single name with the direction indicated by a small arrowhead like this:

This notation is still being used. It is also common to omit the arrowhead. By contrast, the role name notation has a separate name for each of the two directions:

Unfortunately, the direction of each role name is now ambiguous. This is made worse by the fact that there are conflicting definitions of the meaning of the role names in ISO standards. I checked with my contacts at the OMG, and I have determined that the current interpretation of the role names is that both the role name and the cardinality constraint are at the "far end" relative to the subject of the sentence (and at the "near end" relative to the object of the sentence). The conflicting standards have now been superseded. Thus the relationship between Course and Faculty should be read as follows:

  • "Faculty teaches Course" (using the role name "teaches" that is farthest from Faculty) and
  • "Course is taught by Faculty" (using the role name "isTaughtBy" that is farthest from Course)
Similarly, the cardinality constraints are also at the "far end" relative to the subject of the sentence:
  • "A Faculty teaches any number of Courses" (using the cardinality constraint [0..*] that is farthest from Faculty) and
  • "A Course is taught by exactly one Faculty" (using the cardinality constraint [1..1] that is farthest from Course)
