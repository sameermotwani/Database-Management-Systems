Sample Exercise

Design a database for keeping track of standards. A standard has a name, an author (or authors) and a sponsor. A standard consists of a collection of clauses (at least one). Each clause has a text and recommendation level. A recommendation level is one of the following: required, recommended, allowed, discouraged or forbidden. Standards are sponsored by organizations. An organization has a unique name and contact person. An organization may have a parent organization. A person has a name and a unique email address.

Show your design as a UML diagram. Show all cardinality constraints on both associations and attributes.

There are many UML tools available. For a list see Comparison of Unified Modeling Language tools. I have used ArgoUML and BoUML. If you have Visio, then that will also work, but if you do not have Visio, then it is easier to obtain a tool like ArgoUML or BoUML.
