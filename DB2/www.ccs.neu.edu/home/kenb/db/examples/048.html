Sample Exercise

Design a database for countries and their parts. Each country has an official name and may have other names. A country has cities, exactly one of which is the capital. Each city has a population and name. A country is part of one or more continents. Each country is subdivided into parts (such as states or provinces, and the database must keep track of whether the part name is "state", "province",...). Each part is like a country, having cities and a unique capital. Parts can also have subparts, and this can continue to any number of levels. Each part has officials, such as president, governor and mayor. An official is a person, and the same person can play more than one role. A person has at least one name and may have a phone number.

Show your design as a UML diagram. Show all cardinality constraints on both associations and attributes, and label both ends of each association. See UML Role Names for what these labels mean. Use any tool for your diagram, but submit the diagram as an image rather than some specialized file type of your tool. If you cannot get a suitable tool, then draw the diagram on paper, and either submit it to the TA or scan it and email it.
