Cities provide utilities for their homes and business locations. The utilities are water, sewer, electricity and natural gas. Each utility has its own network. Each home, business location and utility network is a part of exactly one city. There may be multiple utility networks of a given type (e.g., there can be several electricity networks), but there is at least one of each type. Each home and business location has an address, and a business location may have a description. A business can have multiple business locations and has a primary business location. Each home and business location may have connections to utility networks. Each utility network has a capacity. The capacity of a utility network is specified using a unit (e.g., the unit for a water utility network could be a cubic foot). Each connection to a utility network has a current usage and at least one purpose. Each city and business has a name.
