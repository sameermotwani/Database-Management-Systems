Grading

The course grade will be based on 5 individual assignments (30%), a group project (20%), and a Final Exam (50%). The Final Exam will be an open-book/open-notes exam. Laptops, PDAs or other communication devices are only permitted at the Final Exam with prior approval. Those permitted to use laptops must sit in the designated areas in the room for this purpose. Normally, the designated areas are on the left and right sides of the room. The Final Exam will be given on 15 and 17 December 2015 during the regular class time in the same room. The Final Exam has a strict 2 hour time limit.

The course grade will be reduced for the following:

  1. Submitting an assignment late (1 point out of 100 for each hour)
  2. Submitting the Final Exam late (1 point out of 60 for each minute)

Extensions

Individual extensions to the deadlines are never given, except for documented medical reasons, in which case a grade of I will be given for the course. Extensions are sometimes given to the class as a whole but only for compelling reasons that are discussed in class well before the deadline.

The Grading Scale can be used to convert from numerical grades to letter grades. However, average grades are computed using the numerical grades. The scale is only used to convert the numerical grade for the course to a letter grade for the course.
